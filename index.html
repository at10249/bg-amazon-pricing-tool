YOUR PRICE
Default selling price — most of the year
  • Hold ≥ 30 days between promo campaigns
  • Must be active to establish the "Was" baseline
  • Ends in .95 — signals standard retail
  • Never promo >50% of any 90-day window
  • Primary PPC bidding price for ACoS targets
SALE / PRICE DISCOUNT
Strike-through "Was → Now" badge
  • Product launch — first 30–60 days
  • Prime Day / Black Friday / major events
  • Clearing slow-moving inventory
  • Min ~5% off; max 30-day campaign (free tool)
  • Ends in .90 — signals promotional pricing
CLEARANCE PRICE
One-time or flash conversion push
  • When sale price isn't converting after 1–2 wks
  • Flash sale / urgent clearance of overstock
  • Temporary competitor price matching only
  • Ends in .97/.88/.77 — signals one-off price
  • Use sparingly — erodes "Was" anchor
COUPON
Green badge visible in search results
  • Visibility without changing listed price
  • Post-launch: need ≥ 10 reviews, ≥ 3.5 stars
  • Does NOT affect Typical Price baseline (good)
  • Cost: $5 flat + 2.5% of attributed sales
  • Stacks with price discounts — math compounds

ACoS TARGET
Advertising Cost of Sale
  • ACoS = ad spend ÷ ad revenue × 100
  • Break-even ACoS = your net margin %
  • Target ACoS must be BELOW net margin
  • 20–30% typical for established products
  • Launch: 50–80% acceptable when buying rank
TACoS TARGET
Total Advertising Cost of Sale
  • TACoS = ad spend ÷ TOTAL revenue (paid+organic)
  • Better measure of true profitability than ACoS
  • Target TACoS 5–10% = healthy organic ratio
  • Falling TACoS over time = organic rank improving
  • Rising TACoS despite stable spend = organic declining
MAX CPC
Maximum Cost Per Click
  • Max CPC = Your Price × Target ACoS% × CVR%
  • Bids above this push ACoS above target
  • Use as hard cap in manual campaigns
  • Start bids at 70% of max CPC, adjust from data
  • Lower CVR = lower max CPC you can afford
m19 STAGES
Tool-specific workflow
  • Stage 1: Force Product Visibility — data collection
  • Stage 2: Monthly Budget — volume building
  • Stage 3: ACoS Target — profitability optimisation
  • Never mix new ASINs with established products
  • Give the AI 1–2 weeks to learn before changing anything
ACoS floor = your net margin %. Once ACoS drops below this, every ad click is profitable. The transition from Launch ACoS to Target ACoS typically takes 8–16 weeks depending on category competition.
These costs are NOT in Amazon's quoted fees. Model them in the inputs — if you don't, actual margins will be lower than shown.
Inbound Shipping (China→FBA): Sea freight ~$0.30–$0.80/unit. Air freight ~$2–$5/unit. Divide total freight cost by units in shipment.
Inbound Placement Fee: If not using Amazon-optimised shipment splits (5+ identical cartons), Amazon charges $0.27–$1.58/unit standard-size. Increased significantly in 2026.
FBA Storage Fees: $0.87/cu ft Jan–Sep, $2.40/cu ft Oct–Dec. Aged inventory surcharge after 181 days: $1.50–$6.90+/unit. Model your sell-through velocity carefully.
Returns Processing: Amazon keeps 20% of referral fee on all returns as admin charge. A 5% return rate can eliminate 1–2% net margin.
PPC / Advertising: New products typically spend $1.50–$4.00/unit blended. Not optional during launch — without PPC you will not rank.
Amazon Vine: $200 flat per ASIN. Target minimum 20 reviews. Vine window ~30 days. Vine-period sales are not real market demand — do not count toward Stage 2 graduation.
Coupon Fees: $5 flat per coupon + 2.5% of attributed sales (cap $2,000). Much cheaper at volume than old $0.60/redemption.
FBA Inbound Defect Fee (2026): Misrouted/abandoned units: $0.32–$1.74 standard-size (was $0.02–$0.07 in 2025). Pack and label carefully.
Overhead: Seller Central $39.99/mo, photography $300–$800 one-time, Brand Registry $500+ legal, tools $50–$100/mo, Vine $200/ASIN. Amortise across annual units.
Two major rule changes hit April–May 2026. Inflated List Prices and persistent discounting to manufacture "Was/Now" badges no longer work reliably.
List Price — April 23, 2026: Must be substantiated by real Amazon sales at that price, OR evidence another retailer sold at that price recently. Amazon removes the strike-through if unverified.
Typical Price — May 18, 2026: If below non-promo median >50% of 90-day window, Amazon includes promo prices in Typical Price. Permanently deflates "Was" until 90 days at higher price.
Price Discount Tool: Free in Seller Central. Needs clean 30-day price history and ~5%+ off. Max 30 days. Creates "Was/Now" badge. Does not appear in Deals section.
Coupon Fee Change — June 2, 2025: Changed from $0.60/redemption to $5 flat + 2.5% of attributed sales (cap $2,000). Recalculate ROI for all coupon campaigns.
FBA Fuel Surcharge — April 17, 2026: 3.5% on top of every FBA fulfillment fee. $3.96 base fee → $4.10. All US/Canada FBA included.
FBA Fee Price Bands — January 15, 2026: Fees differ by 3 price bands: <$10, $10–$50, >$50. Pricing at $9.99 vs $10.00 triggers a different FBA fee tier.

① Calculator
Enter COGS, FBA size tier, target margin and ad costs. The tool calculates your minimum viable list price and shows all four price tiers (List/Your/Sale/Clearance).
② Ad Stages (m19 Methodology)
m19 uses 3 stages: Stage 1 (pure visibility, collect data), Stage 2 (monthly budget, build volume), Stage 3 (ACoS target, step down to profitability). Graduation criteria are shown with live progress.
③ Weekly Check-ins
Record ACoS, ad sales, total revenue and ad spend once per week. The tool tracks TACoS trend and fires kill signals if the product is failing (no sales, ACoS never improving, etc.).
⬆ Import Inventory — Create Products from Amazon
Export the FBA Fee Preview report (Reports → Fulfillment → Fee Preview) and click "Import Inventory" in the top bar. The tool reads ASIN, product name, size tier and weight — then creates product stubs. Add COGS and costs in Edit to complete setup. Products show a yellow dot in the sidebar until done.
How to export:
Seller Central → Reports → Fulfillment → (left sidebar) Fee Preview → Download
Also accepts: Manage Inventory → Download Inventory File (tab-delimited)
④ Import Weekly Check-in Data
In the Check-ins tab, import a Business Report (revenue + CVR) or Advertising Advertised Product Report (ACoS + spend) from Seller Central. The tool auto-detects the report type and matches by ASIN.
